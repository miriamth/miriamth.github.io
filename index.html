ELI FIELDSTEEL

// ABOUT

Dr. Eli Fieldsteel is a composer specializing in music technology with a diverse history of cross-disciplinary collaboration.

Fieldsteel’s music and research engages with the intersection between music technology and contemporary instrumental practice, focusing on topics such as human-computer improvisation,interactivity, and generative music. Fieldsteel is currently Assistant Professor of Music Composition-Theory at The University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, where he serves as the director of the Experimental Music Studios (CV).

Supercollider Tutorials
I maintain a series of SuperCollider video tutorials intended for new users. The full series is available on YouTube. Donations are optional but very much appreciated. If you benefit from these tutorials, please consider donating.

I occasionally live stream my SuperCollider work to Twitch, either as the lecture component of one of my classes, or an in-progress musical work or programming project.
I typically make announcements about content and schedule on Twitter. Topics range from introductory to more advanced ideas like pattern-based music and live coding.

+ New Music
Nosedive (2017)
a composition for wind controller and keyboard controller. Both performers simultaneously control real-time synthesis algorithms running in SuperCollider. The piece is sparsely notated, providing only loose instructions for the performers and inviting long stretches of free improvisation.
Invisible Ink (2016)
an octophonic electroacoustic composition for Wacom tablet and SuperCollider, which treats the pen and tablet as a gestural controller. To generate tension and momentum, the work explores the boundaries between diegetic and non-diegetic sound, as well as linear and non-linear sound spatialization techniques.
Brain Candy (2015)
a quadraphonic improvisation for homemade sensor gloves and SuperCollider. The sensor gloves have a light-sensitive resistor in the palm of each hand, and a 2-axis accelerometer on the back of each hand. A concealed Arduino receives sensor data and sends to SuperCollider via USB. A multi-touch controller provides additional control over global musical parameters.
